  1. Product Quality Standards
    • All products must meet quality standards before being listed for sale.
    • Inspect products for defects, damage, or missing parts before packaging.
    • Report any quality issues to the product manager immediately.
  2. Packaging Materials
    • Use only approved packaging materials for all products.
    • Ensure packaging is clean, undamaged, and appropriate for the product size.
    • Maintain adequate stock of packaging materials at all times.
  3. Packaging Process
    • Follow the standard packaging procedure for each product category.
    • Include all necessary accessories, manuals, and warranty cards.
    • Seal packages securely to prevent tampering during transit.
  4. Labeling Requirements
    • Apply product labels with correct information and pricing.
    • Ensure barcodes are scannable and properly positioned.
    • Include safety warnings and age recommendations where applicable.
  5. Inventory Management
    • Update inventory records immediately after packaging products.
    • Conduct regular inventory checks to ensure accuracy.
    • Report low stock levels to the inventory manager.
  6. Special Handling Instructions
    • Follow special handling instructions for fragile or sensitive products.
    • Use additional protective materials for delicate items.
    • Clearly mark packages containing fragile items.
  7. Returns and Defective Products
    • Inspect returned products to determine the nature of the defect.
    • Document all returns with detailed information about the issue.
    • Follow the established procedure for processing returns and exchanges.
  8. Safety Compliance
    • Ensure all products comply with relevant safety standards and regulations.
    • Stay updated on changes to safety requirements for product categories.
    • Report any safety concerns to the compliance officer immediately.
